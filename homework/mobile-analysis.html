Mobile Browsing - Analysis

Introduction

For this assignment, we were asked to used our mobile devices for any casual browsing for 24 hours. I did not do this. However, I did do this for a few minutes to see which websites were compatible and vice versa.

Questions to consider:

  • Can you still see what you want to see?
  • Can you find what you're looking for?
  • Can you do what you want to do?
  • Any frustrations?
  • Anything work better on the small screen?

Findings

Can you still see what you want to see?

Yes. However, some websites don't even have a mobile version, so I have to scroll in everytime to distinguish the words.

Can you find what you're looking for?

Yes. Albeit, on some mobile versions, the links I click to go into different pages of the websites are located in other places, making it harder to find.

Can you do what you want to do?

As I mentionted above, I can but with more difficulty, whether it is taking time to find what I want, or zooming in constantly

Any frustrations?

Not really.

Anything work better on the small screen?

Twitter. It seems to be very well made for mobile devices.

Summary

I learned that some websites don't have mobile versions, and that websites, like news websites, that need to post a lot of information, can't post as much for me to view, because they usually do one article at a time, that takes up the entire space.
