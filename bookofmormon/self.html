Alma 48

"Moroni...had been preparing the minds of the people to be faithful unto the Lord their God...was a man of perfect understanding...who did labor exceedingly for the welfare and safety of his people...was firm in the faith of Christ...and in their weakest fortifications he did place the greater number of men."

Personal Insights

Especially in the case of Nephi above, God wants us to become our own problem solvers in most cases. Isn't the deepest form of learning out of necessity? Maybe our mortality is made to be the perfect place where problems are designly presented to us, and our progression comes from how we choose to solution or ignore the problems.

We choose here and now who we want to become. And we become by action, our actions directed by our desires.

I get filled with the spirit reading about Moroni's innovation and vision. Clearly, acting on a divine purpose is a great way to learn, as well as feel satisfaction. If we want, we create. The law of the harvest is to teach us satisfaction and self-reliance.
